Assess story sharing planning
label On this page you can answer questions about your community or organization to assess its story sharing culture. Before you answer these questions, you should spend some time listening to people share stories together in the places where they normally gather.
header Narrative freedom
Countering select As you listened to people talk, how often did you hear a person respond to a story with another story that countered it in some way?
Authority select When someone who was obviously in authority was telling stories, how much time and attention did they get?
Mistakes select How many times did you hear people tell stories about mistakes?
Stepping in select When somebody started telling a story and another person stopped them, how did they stop them?
Disagreement select When somebody was telling a story and another person disagreed with the storyteller, how did they disagree?
header Narrative flow
Reminding select When you listened to people telling stories, did you ever hear people say "that reminds me of the time" and then tell a story in response?
Passing on stories select How often did you hear people pass on stories they heard from other people?
Folklore select How much evidence did you find for a narrative folklore in your community or organization?
Story types select Did you hear comic stories, tragic stories, epic stories, and funny stories?
Decision making select Did you ever see people share stories as they prepared to make decisions?
header Narrative knowledge
Recountings of events select Did you see people tell stories that were recountings of events based on emotional experiences from particular perspectives?
Vitality select How lively were the negotiations you heard going on between storytellers and audiences?
Sharing storytelling select Did you ever see two or more people tell a story together?
Blunders select How often did you see someone start telling the wrong story to the wrong people at the wrong time?
Accountability select Did you see people account for their actions and choices by telling each other stories?
header Narrative unity
Common stories select How easy would it be to create a list of stories any member of your community or organization could be expected to know?
Sacred stories select How easy would it be to create a list of sacred stories, those important to understanding the community or organization?
Condensed stories select How easy would it be to create a list of condensed stories, in the form of proverbs or references?
Intermingling select How often were the stories you heard intermingled with each other?
Culture select How easy would it be to describe the unique storytelling culture of your community or organization?
