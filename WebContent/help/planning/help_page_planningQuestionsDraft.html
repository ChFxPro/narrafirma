Planning: Answer PNI Planning Questions
How to complete this page
Brainstorm answers to each of these questions. Write down anything you like about them, and don't censor yourself. This is just your first draft of these answers. You will have a chance to refine them later, after you've done some more planning activities.
Why this page matters
When planning a new project, it's a good idea to think through several important aspects of the project: its goals, relationships, focus, range, scope, and emphasis. If you don't think about these things, you will find it harder to make practical decisions like how and from whom to collect stories.
Questions you might have
What do these terms mean?

Goals: What the project is for; what will be achieved if it succeeds.

Relations: Who will do what in the project - tell stories, listen to stories, make sense of stories, facilitate, and so on.

Focus: The subject matter of the project; its topic.

Range: Perspectives that will be juxtaposed, such as those of two groups or two time frames.

Scope: How many stories will be collected; how many questions will be asked; how many sessions will be held; and so on.

Emphasis: Which of the six phases of PNI (planning, collection, catalysis, sensemaking, intervention, return) will be most important.
