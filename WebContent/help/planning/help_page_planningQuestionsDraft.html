Planning: Answer PNI Planning Questions
Why this page matters
When planning a project, it's a good idea to think through several important aspects of the project: its goals, relationships, focus, range, scope, and emphasis. Thinking about these things will make it easier to make practical decisions later, like how and from whom you will collect stories.
What to do on this page
Brainstorm answers to each of these questions. Write down anything you like. Don't censor yourself. This is only your first draft. You will have a chance to refine your answers later, after you've done some more planning activities.
Questions you might have
What do you mean by goals, relations, etc?
  • Goals: What the project is for; what will be achieved if it succeeds.
  • Relations: Who will do what in the project - tell stories, listen to stories, make sense of stories, facilitate, and so on.
  • Focus: The subject matter of the project; its topic.
  • Range: Varying perspectives that will be juxtaposed, such as those of two groups or two time frames.
  • Scope: How many stories will be collected; how many questions will be asked; how many sessions will be held; and so on.
  • Emphasis: Which of the six phases of PNI (planning, collection, catalysis, sensemaking, intervention, return) will be most important.
For more on the PNI planning questions, see pages 103-105 in Working with Stories.
