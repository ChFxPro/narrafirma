Return

In the return phase of a PNI project, what has been gathered and produced in the first two essential phases (collection and sensemaking) is returned to the community and enters into collective discourse. Such a return may include formal reports and communications, and it may involve a wide or narrow distribution of collected stories. But it is also likely to include informal story exchanges about peopleâ€™s experiences surrounding the project. These informal storytellings may be more influential than the formal outputs, for better or worse, and they merit attention on their own terms. All PNI projects involve someone telling stories they would not have told before the project took place. Whether this is a stated goal of the project or not, it always happens, because the project is itself a story that takes place in the community. Conscious attention to the return phase is a mark of projects that have lasting positive impacts.

This NarraFirma page lists the return pages of your project and shows the reminders you wrote about them.

The return pages are:

  • Gather feedback
  • Reflect on the project
  • Prepare outline of project presentation
  • Respond to requests for post-project support
