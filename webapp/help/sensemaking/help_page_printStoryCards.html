Print story cards

On this page you can print story cards to be used in sensemaking sessions.

What to do here

Choose a story collection, then click the Print Story Cards button. The cards will appear in a separate window, and you can print them from your browser.

Frequently-asked questions

A few tips on changing slider characters:

  • Some characters are wider than others. If your before-the-button character is wider than your after-the-button character, your slider values may appear different than they really are.
  • White space is collapsed in HTML. This means you can use an empty space for your after-the-button character, but you can't use it before the button.
  • You can use emojis and other special characters, but whether your browser and printer will print them correctly is anybody's guess. See what works for you.

How do I use the "Custom CSS" feature?

Click "Print Story Cards," then use your browser to inspect any element of your story cards you want to change. (The usual way to do this is to right-click on a word or area and choose "Inspect" or "Inspect Element" from the popup menu that appears. If you don't see a popup menu or an "Inspect" menu item, look up how to inspect a web page element in the browser you are using. Sometimes you need to turn on "developer" mode to inspect page elements.)

When you have successfully inspected the page element you want to change, you should see its CSS class. Most of the classes start with "narrafirma-story-card".

Next go to the "custom CSS" field on the the "Print story cards" page and write a class selector (label) and declaration (set of formatting lines) for that class. For example, if you wanted all choice-question selections (drop-down list, checkboxes, radio buttons) to appear in blue, you would write a CSS statement like this:

.narrafirma-story-card-select-selected, .narrafirma-story-card-checkboxes-selected, .narrafirma-story-card-radiobuttons-selected {
    color: blue;
}

Then reload your browser and print your story cards again to see your change. (You can't just reload the story cards page; you have to reload the NarraFirma application, then click the "Print Story Cards" button again.)
